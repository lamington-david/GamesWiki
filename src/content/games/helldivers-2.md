## Design Notes

**A human Game Master as a live design system.** The most structurally unusual element of HELLDIVERS 2 is not its gunplay or progression — it's Joel. Arrowhead employs a developer whose entire job is to run the galactic war like a tabletop dungeon master: choosing which major orders go live, deciding when and where the Automatons or Terminids attack, adjusting planet difficulty in real time, and occasionally issuing special stratagems to individual players mid-mission to add surprise. This turns what would typically be a scripted event system into a live creative performance, giving the metagame an improvisational quality that no algorithm could produce.

**"Sandcastles" — designing for the cycle of agency and challenge.** Pilestedt describes the intended player experience as: build your sandcastle, then have Joel stomp on it. Players are given enough agency in the galactic war to feel genuine ownership of victories — planets liberated, campaigns won, major orders completed. These victories are then systematically challenged by Joel's interventions. This rhythm creates investment that persistent success would undermine: if players always won, the wins would become meaningless. The deliberate imposition of failure is what makes the co-op victories feel like they matter.

**Fundamentals before features.** Pilestedt's design philosophy, articulated publicly at GDC, is that studios make their biggest mistakes when they prioritize features over fundamentals. Fundamentals are: what is the core fantasy the game delivers, who is it for, and how does it feel at baseline. Style is how that raw material is directed into a coherent experience. Features emerge from those two foundations. A game that adds features before establishing fundamentals produces something he describes as "a car designed for everyone" — too broad to be excellent at anything. HELLDIVERS 2's tight third-person shooter core and specific "co-op military satire" identity reflect this framework: everything else is decoration on a clearly defined base.

**Co-op social design through shared vulnerability.** Unlike many co-op games where players are simply additive — more players means more firepower — HELLDIVERS 2 designs around shared vulnerability. Friendly fire is on by default, stratagems can be misaimed, and orbital strikes kill teammates as readily as enemies. This creates a social contract around competence and communication that most co-op games avoid. The payoff is that successful missions feel genuinely collaborative: you had to coordinate, communicate, and trust each other to survive, rather than just all shooting at the same target.

**Managing live service as a creative act.** HELLDIVERS 2's massive launch success created immediate pressure on Arrowhead's live service infrastructure and update cadence. Pilestedt has spoken publicly about the tension between delivering content quickly enough to retain players and maintaining enough quality control to avoid damaging the game's feel. His conclusion — that stability and quality must never be sacrificed for speed — reflects a recognition that a live service game's most important asset is player trust, which can be lost faster than it can be rebuilt through content updates.

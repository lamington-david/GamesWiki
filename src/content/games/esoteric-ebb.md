## Design Notes

**The Dungeon Master as solo designer.** Christoffer Bodegård spent eight years building Esoteric Ebb alone, and the game's design sensibility is rooted in his tabletop experience. His framing of the role of a Dungeon Master as "wrestling with players for agency" shapes how the game handles choice — it gives the player considerable freedom while the world consistently asserts its own rules and politics. This tension between authored intention and player improvisation is the game's core dramatic engine.

**D&D 5e rules as CRPG scaffold.** Rather than inventing a bespoke system, Bodegård built Esoteric Ebb on recognisable D&D 5th Edition mechanics — classes, ability checks, skill rolls — and tuned them to replace combat with dialogue. This decision lowered design overhead on the systemic layer while freeing creative energy for the writing. Familiar mechanical vocabulary means players spend cognitive effort on story choices rather than learning new rules, a division of complexity that makes the game's 1.25 million words feel navigable rather than overwhelming.

**Scope discipline in solo development.** Building a game of this scale alone required Bodegård to develop unusually rigorous writing habits — reportedly 2,000 to 5,000 words per day over years. The design reflects this: rather than wide-branching plots that multiply exponentially, Esoteric Ebb uses a political city-mystery structure that funnels player choices toward legible consequences within a contained geography. Scoping the world tightly while expanding it vertically through dialogue depth is a transferable lesson in sustainable solo development.

**Political narrative without didacticism.** The central conspiracy involves corruption in Norvik's democratic institutions, but the game avoids lecturing by placing the player inside factional conflicts rather than above them. Bodegård's approach — taking inspiration from Scandinavian folklore and post-war political history — gives the world a specific texture that makes its themes feel earned rather than grafted on. Concreteness of setting serves as an anchor for otherwise abstract political themes.

**The legacy of Disco Elysium for CRPGs.** Esoteric Ebb belongs to a small wave of games reinterpreting what a CRPG can be after Disco Elysium demonstrated that mechanical depth and literary ambition are compatible. Bodegård explicitly cites it as an influence, and the decision to centralise dialogue over combat reflects the same wager: that players who come for the story will also find the system gratifying, and that a strong RPG premise doesn't require violence as its primary skill-expression venue.

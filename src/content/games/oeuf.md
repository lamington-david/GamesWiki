## Design Notes

**Object as design specification.** Oeuf's central decision — the protagonist is an egg — is not a cosmetic choice. The egg's physical properties are the game's entire movement design: it rolls smoothly when lying on its side, bounces chaotically when balanced on its narrow end, and responds to inclines in ways that differ dramatically depending on orientation. increpare didn't need to design a movement system and then assign a character to represent it; choosing the character gave him the movement system. Every level, challenge, and expression of player skill follows from the physical truth of an egg. This is design economy at its most disciplined — one object decision generates everything else.

**Physics honesty as design commitment.** increpare has spoken extensively about "honest" game design: games that communicate their rules accurately and don't deceive or exploit players. Oeuf applies this philosophy to physics — the egg behaves consistently according to its simulation, not according to what would be convenient for the player. When the egg rolls unexpectedly, it is because the physics were applied consistently, not because the game cheated. This commitment creates a demanding skill ceiling but also a clear learning path: players who fail can always identify what went wrong, because the system's rules are never hidden. Honest difficulty and fair difficulty are, in increpare's design, the same thing.

**Cozy aesthetics with challenging design.** Oeuf pairs unforgiving physics mastery with a deliberately gentle presentation: constant rain sounds, a meditative ambient soundtrack running to four hours of music, no enemies, no timers, and no punitive death feedback. The aesthetic removes frustration as the primary emotional response to failure — losing ground on a difficult section feels like a meditation retreat setback rather than a competitive defeat. This contrast between soft presentation and demanding design is deliberate. Difficulty and aggression are not synonyms, and the most demanding skill games can be emotionally peaceful. Oeuf is an argument for that claim.

**The level editor as design philosophy.** Oeuf ships with a fully featured level editor and Steam Workshop support, which means the game's design doesn't end at the credits. Players who master the physics can build challenges that the developer could not anticipate, extending the creative system beyond what a solo developer could produce in a single release. A robust level editor is only possible when the underlying design is coherent enough that its rules are communicable to builders — it signals that increpare understood the egg's physics well enough to hand those tools to others with confidence. The Workshop support is both a player feature and a proof of design clarity.

**Solo development and design focus.** increpare has made hundreds of small experimental games over a long career — a discipline that produces designers capable of identifying exactly what a game needs and nothing more. Oeuf contains no collectibles, no upgrade trees, no cosmetic shops, no padded content. It is as long as it needs to be with exactly the design surface the idea requires. Solo development creates the conditions for this kind of focus: no publisher requiring more content, no team requiring additional features to justify their roles. The absence of extraneous design is not a limitation of Oeuf's budget; it is a deliberate statement about what the game is for.

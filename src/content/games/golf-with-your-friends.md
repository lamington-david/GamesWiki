## Design Notes

**Simultaneous play as a design foundation.** Golf With Your Friends allows up to 12 players to play every hole at the same time, abandoning the turn-based format of traditional miniature golf. This single decision transforms the game's social dynamic: waiting is eliminated, everyone is engaged throughout, and the chaos of multiple balls on the same course simultaneously generates emergent comedy and shared moments. The design lesson is that multiplayer pacing shapes social experience as powerfully as content does — a game where players are watching rather than playing has a fundamentally different feel than one where everyone acts at once, regardless of how good the content itself is.

**Course design as escalating legibility.** Blacklight Interactive's design principle for each course is that holes must escalate in difficulty across the map, with every challenge immediately visible to players rather than hidden. New players should always understand what they are trying to do, even if they cannot yet do it. Hidden trick shots are layered in for experienced players without obscuring the primary route. This approach — obvious path, discoverable depth — mirrors best practices from level design in platformers and puzzle games, applied to a context where players of mixed skill levels compete simultaneously and must all remain engaged. A hole that confuses new players fails even if it rewards experienced ones.

**Community-driven level creation.** The Twilight course in Golf With Your Friends was designed through a community process in which players submitted hole designs during developer livestreams and the team built them live. This model produced content that players felt ownership over and that reflected what the community actually found fun rather than what developers assumed they would. The community-driven approach also served as free market research — the enthusiasm or absence of enthusiasm for particular hole types during streams gave the team real-time feedback on which design directions had the most support. Making development visible to the community generated both better content and stronger community investment in the game's success.

**Self-taught development and learning in public.** Blacklight Interactive's founders — Kailan, Brayden, and Lachlan Clark — had no formal game development education and taught themselves through YouTube tutorials and online resources during development. Golf With Your Friends was built in Early Access over four years before its full release in 2020, with the development process conducted in public. This approach meant the game was continuously refined against real player feedback rather than internal assumptions. The self-taught origin is not just a backstory — it reflects a development methodology that prioritised learning what was working in practice over applying theoretical frameworks about what should work.

**Physics customisation as social amplifier.** Golf With Your Friends allows players to modify ball shape, size, collision behaviour, gravity, and friction, enabling configurations far outside standard mini-golf rules. These modifications serve a specific social function: they generate situations that would not occur in a conventionally designed game, producing shared moments of absurdity that become the stories players tell about the session afterward. This is a design strategy for social replay value — by giving players tools to generate their own chaos, the game produces emergent content that no fixed level design could anticipate. The physics system is not just customisation; it is a social comedy generator.
